About Dr. Prajapati

Dedicated to Protecting Your Most Precious Sense

Dr. Kiran B. Prajapati, M.Optom
Specialist Optometrist | Pediatric Vision Expert | Healthcare Entrepreneur

He is an evidence-based Optometrist specializing in pediatric vision and accessible healthcare. Holding an M.Optom from Shree K. P. Sanghvi Eye Institute and a B.Optom from Rotary Eye Institute, he combines clinical excellence with a mission to serve all segments of society.

Clinical Experience & Expertise
Following roles at Netra Jyot Eye Hospital and Dr. Devang Naik Hospital, he founded Div's Eye Care in 2021 to provide high-quality, affordable service. He also serves as a Consultant Optometrist at Kids Care Hospital, Navsari.

Comprehensive Diagnostics: Vision testing, ocular disease management, contact lens fitting, Orthoptics, Low Vision rehabilitation, and Ocular Prosthetics.

Pediatric Focus: Specializes in visual development and behavioral optometry. He actively counsels parents on digital screen management, study ergonomics, and preventive visual hygiene.

Community Impact
Serving as a Trustee of the Ma Manav Seva Charitable Trust, he has organized over 200 eye check-up camps, providing free screenings, subsidized spectacles, and surgical assistance.

What is Vision Therapy?

Vision Therapy is a clinically supervised, evidence-based programme that retrains the brain-eye connection. Unlike glasses, which compensate for visual errors, Vision Therapy addresses the underlying cause — building lasting improvements in eye teaming, tracking, focus, and visual processing.

"Vision is not just about seeing 20/20. It's about how the brain processes what the eyes see — and that's what Vision Therapy changes." — Dr. Kiran Prajapati
For Children

Amblyopia, strabismus, convergence insufficiency, and reading difficulties. Early intervention yields the best outcomes.

For Adults

Post-concussion visual syndrome, digital eye strain, sports vision, and binocular vision dysfunction.

Proven Results

CITT research confirms 73–88% success rate for Convergence Insufficiency — superior to glasses alone.
